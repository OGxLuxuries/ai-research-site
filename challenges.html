14 Social Topics for discussion

  1. 01
  2. Outputs from data processing do not represent the world but construct it, meaning AI systems cannot generate knowledge that accurately captures an independent reality.
  3. 02
  4. Methods of supervised Machine Learning perpetuate existing regularities, hindering social change by neglecting discontinuities or irritations, thus presenting an obstacle to encouraging change.
  5. 03
  6. Software developers often lack specific knowledge about the areas where Machine Learning applications are used, leading to poor assessment of technological consequences; this challenge can be solved with enhanced contextual knowledge.
  7. 04
  8. AI systems reflect the values and assumptions of their developers, making the existence of a value-free, neutral, and objective AI impossible.
  9. 05
  10. The high degree of automation in developing intelligent information technologies might alleviate the current shortage of expert knowledge and talent needed for AI systems.
  11. 06
  12. Social acceptance of AI technologies is currently limited, but in the future, broader application areas might become feasible even where social acceptance is lacking today.
  13. 07
  14. The alleged automation achieved by AI systems comes with "invisible" costs, including precarious work conditions and environmental impacts.
  15. 08
  16. The operation of AI systems is complex infrastructurally and materially, posing uncertainties about the stability, energy, and natural resources future societies can provide for widespread AI implementation.
  17. 09
  18. Presently, significant gaps exist between artificial and natural intelligence, but in the future, AI technologies could potentially function more closely to biological brains.
  19. 10
  20. Explainability is a current challenge as many AI applications are too complex for human understanding; future progress may lead to AI systems providing understandable reasons for their outputs.
  21. 11
  22. Current supervised Machine Learning technologies are often fixed on specific application areas, and future AI systems might achieve flexibility to solve tasks in various domains with less susceptibility to malfunctions.
  23. 12
  24. Many Machine Learning methods rely on limited-sized data sets, and this challenge could be addressed in future AI technologies, for example, by utilizing unsupervised Machine Learning techniques.
  25. 13
  26. Supervised Machine Learning often requires hand-labeled training data sets lacking complexity; this challenge is solvable as AI is moving towards techniques that reduce the need for extensive training data sets.
  27. 14
  28. AI systems can be used for both defense and offensive cyber-attacks, and achieving complete security for AI-based information technologies to eliminate vulnerabilities is considered impossible.
