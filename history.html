From 1950 - 2023

1950

Alan Turing releases "Computing Machinery and Intelligence," where the renowned codebreaker of the Nazi ENIGMA cipher during WWII endeavors to address the query of whether machines can possess cognitive abilities. Turing introduces the Turing Test in an attempt to ascertain if a computer can exhibit comparable intelligence, or at least produce results equivalent to human intelligence. The significance of the Turing test has been a topic of ongoing debate.

1956

John McCarthy coins the term 'artificial intelligence' at the first-ever AI conference at Dartmouth College. Logic Theorist is created, the first-ever running AI software program.

1980s

Neural networks which use a backpropagation algorithm to train itself become widely used in AI applications.

1997

IBM's Deep Blue beats then world chess champion Garry Kasparov, in a chess match (and rematch).

2016

DeepMind's AlphaGo program, powered by a deep neural network, beats Lee Sodol, the world champion Go player, in a five-game match. The victory is significant given the huge number of possible moves as the game progresses (over 14.5 trillion after just four moves).

2023

ChatGPT brings about a significant transformation in the performance of AI and its capacity to enhance enterprise value. Through innovative generative AI approaches, deep-learning models can undergo pre-training using extensive volumes of unprocessed, unlabeled data.
