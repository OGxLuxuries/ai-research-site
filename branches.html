Classes & Applications of A.I.

Logical AI

This branch involves representing a program's knowledge, facts about the world, and goals using sentences in a mathematical logical language, and decision-making is based on logical inference

Search AI

Programs in this branch explore numerous possibilities, such as chess moves or theorem-proving inferences, continuously improving efficiency across various domains.

Pattern Recognition

AI programs compare observations, like recognizing patterns in vision or language, employing different methods for simple and complex patterns (e.g., in chess positions) that require distinct approaches.

Representation and Inference

The representation of world facts using mathematical logic and drawing inferences, including non-monotonic reasoning, where conclusions can be reversed based on evidence, contribute to AI

Learning from Experience

AI programs learn from experience through approaches like connectionism, neural nets, or logical expressions, but limitations exist due to the formalism's ability to represent information

Brute Force Computation

In game playing applications, machines achieve master-level chess performance through brute force computation, examining hundreds of thousands of positions to beat opponents, even world champions.

Computer Vision

AI faces challenges in computer vision as the world, composed of three-dimensional objects, is captured by two-dimensional inputs from human eyes and computer cameras. Representing three-dimensional information directly remains limited, hindering full computer vision capabilities.
